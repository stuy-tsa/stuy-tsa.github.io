TEAMS

The Test of Engineering Aptitude, Mathematics, and Science, or TEAMS, is an annual competition that pits the top STEM students in the nationa against each other in a breakneck race to solve a problem.

About

TEAMS is an annual competition that gives students an opportunity to discover engineering and how knowledge in engineering can help make a difference in the world. Students work collaboratively to solve real-world engineering challenges, applying their math and science skills in practical, creative ways.
TEAMS offers:

  • Exciting, theme-based academic competition
  • Real-world applications of math and science
  • Collaborative skill building for teams of 4 to 8 students
  • A two part competition, including multiple choice and short answer essay questions
  • Participation recognition of all teams
  • Certificates and awards for division, state and national winners

 

Format of Competition

Team members work in a written competition format in teams of four to eight students competing for competition day, division and state awards.

Part 1 Eighty multiple choice questions, requiring team members to apply math and science knowledge to novel situations. To be completed in 90 minutes.

Part 2 Five tasks to be answered in a short essay format, expanding students' ideas as they explain their ideas for engineering solutions. To be completed in 90 minutes.

 

Dates and Figures

  • Online registration begins in September each year.
  • State-wide competitions take place during February and March.
  • Students compete at their own school, at a neighboring school, or at a nearby university host site.
  • Top-ranking teams are invited to compete for “Best in Nation” honors at the national TSA conference in June.

About TSA

The Technology Student Association (TSA) is a national student organization devoted exclusively to the needs of students engaged in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM). Open to students enrolled in or who have completed technology education courses, TSA’s membership includes over 200,000 middle and high school students in approximately 2,000 schools spanning 49 states.
